Fake Call Center Scam: 25 Arrested in Noida Raid

By Vikram Singh | October 13, 2025 | 3:00 AM IST

Noida Police busted a fake call center operation that was duping US citizens by posing as IRS officials. The raid led to the arrest of 25 people and seizure of computers, phones, and call scripts.

The call center was operating from a commercial building in Sector 63. Employees would call US residents claiming they owed taxes and threatening legal action if immediate payment wasn't made.

The scam came to light when FBI alerted Indian authorities about complaints from American citizens. The operation had been running for eight months and had defrauded victims of approximately $2 million.

"They had detailed scripts and training manuals on how to intimidate victims," said SP Cyber Crime. "Most employees were young graduates who were promised high salaries."

The mastermind, who is still absconding, had set up similar operations in other cities. Police are coordinating with international agencies to track money trails and identify more victims.
