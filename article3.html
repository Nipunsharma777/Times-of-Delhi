Drug Trafficking Network Exposed in Mumbai

By Amit Verma | October 13, 2025 | 5:00 AM IST

Mumbai Police's Narcotics Control Bureau busted a major drug trafficking network operating across Maharashtra. The operation led to the seizure of 50 kg of heroin worth Rs 150 crores and arrest of 12 individuals.

The network was smuggling drugs from international borders and distributing them through a sophisticated supply chain in major cities. Police conducted simultaneous raids at 15 locations across Mumbai, Pune, and Nashik.

The kingpin, identified as Salman Sheikh, had been operating the network for over five years. He used encrypted messaging apps and cryptocurrency for transactions to avoid detection.

"This is one of the biggest drug busts in recent years," said DCP Narcotics. "The network had connections with international drug cartels."

Police also seized luxury cars, cash worth Rs 2 crores, and fake documents. The accused have been booked under the NDPS Act and remanded to judicial custody.
