Investment Scam: Ponzi Scheme Mastermind Caught

By Amit Verma | October 12, 2025 | 8:00 PM IST

Economic Offences Wing arrested the mastermind behind a Rs 200 crore Ponzi scheme that duped thousands of investors across North India. The accused, Rajiv Malhotra, promised returns of 30% annually through his fake investment company.

Malhotra operated through a network of agents who recruited investors with promises of guaranteed high returns. The scheme collapsed when he failed to pay returns and investors filed complaints.

Investigation revealed that Malhotra used new investors' money to pay earlier investors, a classic Ponzi scheme pattern. He diverted crores to personal accounts and purchased luxury properties abroad.

"He lived a lavish lifestyle while investors lost their life savings," said Joint Commissioner EOW. "We have frozen his bank accounts and attached properties worth Rs 50 crores."

Over 5,000 investors have filed complaints. Police are working to recover funds and return money to victims. Malhotra faces charges of cheating, criminal breach of trust, and money laundering.
